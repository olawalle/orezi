Amplify

Overview

Amplified is a bible app that lets users read the bible both online and offline, it allows for multiple verses of different passages to be read at a go and users can add prayers to a verse. The app also has audio play; this lets users play prayers, verses or scriptures of their choosing. This app already existed in the google store and has a number of users. The main goal of this project was to redesign the existing app and take into consideration the pain points of users and create a better experience that is minimalistic and aesthetically pleasing.

My Role

My role included; user research, branding, information architecture, wireframing and visual design.

Design Process

User Research

My first step was to download the existing app and do a design qualitative analysis of the existing app, I also went through users comments and complaints, this helped me get a better understanding. I also did an interview with five of their users to gain better insight. From these above I was able to go further to create my user personas.

Existing App

These were the existing screens, I decided that I was going to change most of it but maintain some parts of it such as rounded corners, the ux was totally off, therefore I had to create a new user map and personas.

User Persona

I craeted new user personas because I had gained a lot from the research. The personas captures different goals and needs. It also shows their frustrations and personality this helped decision making.

User Map

Due to the type of product, we have two very different distinctive types of users and had to create user maps for both, while figuring out how they both connect and interact.

Wireframes

Wireframes were designed and prototyped, then given to users to test, it was also reviewed by some team members and the product owner after which, changes were made and the full fidelity design was created.

Visual Design

The first version of the app was just for andriod, but this version was a hybrid app, so that users are able to have same experience even on different operating systems.

Onboarding

The onboarding screen has three options, to sign in for existing users or sign up for new users, and finally the option to skip. The option to skip is important especially for users who do not like to sign up, the screen after choosing to skip explains that choice and what it will mean for the user.

Home, Bible & Listening

The home screen is basically a dashboard showing all your recents and access to everything including profile and other features. The Bible lets you highlight when a verse is held down, this method was used because from the research carried out, users were familiar with this method.

Multiple, Prayers & Listen

Multiple lets users add various verses from all over the bible, name it and can read or listen to them in one loop. Prayers can be created and added to a verse, it can also be created as a stand alone, and either be read or listened to. Prayers can also be added to a multiple loop.

Conclusion

Redesigning an existing app can be tricky because users are already used to a certain experience and in this situation we were not only adding new features but we were revamping the whole thing. The lesson here is that even if users are used to a certain experience they will want a better one especially if the familiar experience is a bad one.
