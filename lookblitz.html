Lookblitz.

The platform for booking and buying beauty services and products.

About

Lookblitz is a web app that lets users book for beauty and fashion services, shop from items from said store and on another end it lets users (sellers) registe and create a store front where they can add services that they offer such as hair dressing, massages e.t.c, they can also put up products that they sell and can be shipped. This platform is an all in one for a business owner because it lets them create their own niche in a space where they can easily be found, upload pictures to showcase their works and aslo see what their competitors are doing. On the other hand, it lets users (shoppers) search for services based on location and this causes a ripple effect of time saving. It also lets users (shoppers) view reviews on the services rendered and compare prices, distance and comfort level at a glance.

My Role

My role included, ux strategy, information architecture, wireframing, visual design and prototyping

Design Process

User Research

I joined the team at a point where they had already gone through the process of user research, so I had the data I needed to start my work.

User Persona

Using my personal user persona template, I created three different personas; with two being consumers (buyers or shoppers on the web app) and one being a service provider or seller. This helped to streamline the different flows that both users will experience, as they are both on the same app but having totally different experiencies.

User Map

Due to the type of product, we have two very different distinctive types of users and had to create user maps for both, while figuring out how they both connect and interact.

Final Version

After a lot of reviews and back and forth, we decided (I and the other designer) to start the user experience from scratch, this was excluding the user personas and maps because they worked. We really wanted the experience to be seamless, and we could already see where it was lacking in the designs we had created above. We also had a lot of concerns about how dominant the color (E0115F) was excessively used, it made it uncomfortable to use, admittedly we chose the color because we were more focused on sellers/service givers in the beauty and fashion industry.

Testing

Conclusion

Creating an application that has two users can become tedious but having good research and a team to work with makes it a lot easier. My major takeaway is that users are who we are designing for and keeping their goals and experience when designing is the most important part of the job.
